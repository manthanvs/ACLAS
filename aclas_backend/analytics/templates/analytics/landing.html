How It Works

From typing to tracking — in 5 seamless steps

1

Your Editor Keeps an Eye Out

Our extension runs quietly in your IDE. It notices things like file switches, repeated compiler errors, undo keystrokes, and whether you are actively typing or idling.

2

Sending Telemetry Packets

The extension securely batches these tiny state updates and sends them over HTTPS utilizing your unique personal API token.

3

Safe Storage

The centralized backend receives your data, sanitizes it, and saves it onto a secure private database linked solely to you.

4

Evaluating Stress Vectors

The system constantly assesses recent events. A complex formula weighs code failures, repeated errors, and undo habits to calculate a live Cognitive "Stress Score".

5

Your Personal Dashboard

Your raw data is presented cleanly on your Dashboard—exposing your peak productive hours, identifying problematic projects, and showing exact stress spikes.

Evaluation Algorithms

We normalize chaos across time.

ACLAS utilizes a time-decaying point system. Every distinct negative event type contributes to an accumulator. To ensure fairness, total points are divided by your active coding duration—so naturally longer sessions aren't unfairly penalized. The gauge is finally capped at 100%.

1. Point System: Errors + Repeated Errors(x2) + Build Fails(x3)...
2. Rate Normalization: Base Points / Max(1, ActiveHours) * TuningConstant.
3. Maximum Clamping: min(CalculatedScore, 100).
